# International Visitor Numbers (2016–2025)
The graph illustrates the number of international visitors, measured in millions, to the USA, France, the UK, and Italy between 2016 and 2025.
Overall, France remained the most visited country throughout the period, while the UK consistently recorded the lowest visitor numbers.
Another major feature of the graph is the sharp decline in tourism during 2020 and 2021, which was most likely caused by the Covid-19 pandemic.
After this period, all four countries experienced a gradual recovery.
Between 2016 and 2019, tourism generally increased in all countries shown on the graph.
France rose steadily from approximately 83 million visitors in 2016 to around 90 million in 2019.
Similarly, the USA increased from about 77 million to nearly 80 million visitors during the same period.
Italy also showed continuous growth, climbing from just over 50 million visitors in 2016 to approximately 65 million in 2019.
In contrast, the UK remained relatively stable, fluctuating between 37 and 40 million visitors.
However, international tourism fell dramatically in 2020.
France dropped from around 90 million visitors to approximately 35 million.
The USA experienced a similar decline, falling to just under 20 million visitors.
Italy also saw a sharp decrease, while the UK recorded the lowest figure on the graph at around 11 million visitors.
Tourism continued to struggle in 2021, especially in the UK, where visitor numbers fell even further to approximately 7 million.
From 2022 onwards, the tourism industry began to recover steadily.
France showed the strongest recovery, reaching around 100 million visitors by 2023 and approximately 110 million by 2025.
The USA also recovered significantly and exceeded 80 million visitors by the end of the period.
Italy followed a similar trend, increasing gradually to around 70 million visitors in 2025.
Although the UK recovered more slowly than the other countries, visitor numbers still rose to just above 40 million by the end of the graph.
In conclusion, the graph clearly demonstrates the severe impact of the Covid-19 pandemic on international tourism, as well as the gradual recovery that followed in the years afterwards.
