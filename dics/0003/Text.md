# Addressing the Reading Crisis
The decline in reading habits has become a serious concern in many countries.
Reading is not only a form of entertainment but also a key skill that supports education, professional development and critical thinking.
However, many people today prefer short digital content such as social media posts and videos instead of longer texts.
As a result, fewer people engage with books on a regular basis, which may weaken literacy skills and reduce productivity in the workplace.
This report proposes three measures that could help address the reading crisis.
One possible solution is to increase access to digital books.
Governments could introduce subsidies to make electronic books very affordable or even free for a limited period of time.
This would remove financial barriers and allow more people to explore literature while still supporting authors and publishers.
Another important measure would be promoting literature through modern digital platforms.
Short videos, quotes and story fragments could be shared on social media to attract new readers.
These short pieces of content could serve as an introduction to books and encourage people to discover full stories.
A further strategy could involve introducing reward-based reading systems.
Digital platforms could track reading progress and provide points, badges or other incentives.
Such systems may motivate people to read more regularly and make engagement with books more enjoyable.
In conclusion, the reading crisis can be addressed through improved access to books, modern promotion strategies and innovative engagement systems.
If implemented effectively, these measures could help rebuild a strong and sustainable reading culture.
